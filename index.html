About Me

I'm Oliver Cole — a mature Computer Science (Games Development) student at the University of Plymouth and an aspiring game developer. After several years in finance, I decided to pursue games professionally, bringing together technical problem-solving, creative design, and a strong interest in building interactive systems that feel rewarding to engage with.

My recent work has focused on both game development and graphics programming. I am currently developing a 3D logic-gate puzzle game in Godot for my COMP3000 final year project, combining puzzle design, environmental interaction, and core computing concepts within a more playful office-based setting. Alongside this, I have been creating coursework in C++ and OpenGL, exploring areas such as lighting, shaders, materials, post-processing, and immersive 3D scene design.

Across these projects, I enjoy rapid prototyping, iterative playtesting, and refining ideas over time to make them stronger both technically and creatively. I am particularly interested in gameplay systems that encourage experimentation, clear player feedback, and a sense of progression through interaction and discovery.

A lot of my inspiration comes from cinematic and adventure-driven games, especially experiences that combine strong atmosphere, exploration, and satisfying mechanics. I am especially drawn to projects that let me blend technical implementation with game feel, whether that is through puzzle systems, player interaction, or visual presentation.

  • Current focus: Godot (GDScript) — building a 3D logic-gate puzzle game for my COMP3000 final year project
  • Graphics work: C++ and OpenGL — developing coursework focused on rendering, lighting, shaders, and 3D scene presentation
  • Other work: C++/SDL — creating a top-down sci-fi survival shooter with wave-based gameplay and rescue mechanics

I'm always looking to keep improving as a developer, both through larger long-term projects and smaller experimental ones.
